## Process Maturity Self-Rating

Circle or mark one:

| Rating | What it means |
|--------|---------------|
| **Early** | I'm mostly winging it. Sessions are unstructured, docs are sparse or nonexistent, and I rarely review what I've built. |
| **Developing** | I have some habits forming — session notes, a backlog — but they're inconsistent. I lose context between sessions. |
| **Strong** | I have consistent session discipline, maintained docs, a working backlog, and I review my process regularly. |
| **Highly Disciplined** | My process is fully documented, prompts are reused, decisions are logged, and I can hand this project to anyone with full context. |

## Prompt: How to generate this document

```
I want to reflect on my recent development work and fill out a self-assessment worksheet. Help me answer each question honestly by asking me reflective questions about my project.

Here is my project context:
- Project name: [PROJECT NAME]
- Primary AI tools I use: [LIST TOOLS]
- What I'm building: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION]
- How long I've been working on it: [DURATION]

Please ask me the following questions one at a time, wait for my response, and then help me turn my answers into clear, specific worksheet entries:

1. What is the most expensive mistake you've made on this project so far — in time, money, or rework — and what would have prevented it?
2. What is one assumption you held early on that turned out to be wrong or that you should have challenged before acting on it?
3. Think about work you did in small pieces across multiple sessions or tool switches. What is one task that would have been faster or cheaper if you'd done it all at once?
4. What is one deployment or production risk that exists right now in your project that isn't written down anywhere?
5. Looking at the book's rules, which one resonates most with where you've been falling short? What will you do differently in your very next session?
6. Rate your current process maturity honestly: Early, Developing, Strong, or Highly Disciplined — and explain why.
7. Name three specific, concrete changes you'll make this week. "Be more disciplined" doesn't count — what exactly will you do?

After collecting my answers, format them as a completed worksheet ready to save.
```

## Prompt: How to update this document

```
I want to review and update my vibe-to-value self-assessment worksheet. It has been approximately [one month / one quarter] since I last completed it.

Here is my current worksheet:
[PASTE WORKSHEET HERE]

Here is what has happened since I last filled this out:
- What I've shipped or built: [DESCRIPTION]
- Process changes I actually made: [LIST]
- Any new expensive mistakes or near-misses: [DESCRIPTION]
- How my tool usage has changed: [DESCRIPTION]

Please help me update the worksheet by:
1. Reviewing whether my three committed changes from last time were actually completed — and noting what happened with each
2. Identifying whether any of my previous risks have materialized or been resolved
3. Re-evaluating my process maturity rating based on the evidence from the past period
4. Helping me identify a new "most expensive mistake" or "should have batched" item from recent work
5. Setting three new specific changes for the coming week

Ask me any clarifying questions you need before writing the update. The goal is an honest, current snapshot — not a polished self-presentation.
```
